On Shopify, we have exactly 100 sneaker shops, and each of these shops sells only one model of shoe. We want to do some analysis of the average order value (AOV). When we look at orders data over a 30 day window, we naively calculate an AOV of $3145.13. Given that we know these shops are selling sneakers, a relatively affordable item, something seems wrong with our analysis.

Brief answers to the questions with explanations below:

a. Think about what could be going wrong with our calculation. Think about a better way to evaluate this data. -Some orders have extremely large amounts of items or are selling the sneakers for extremely large amounts b. What metric would you report for this dataset? -Average sneaker price across stores, average number of sneakers sold throughout the month per store c. What is its value? -Average sneaker price: 152.26 -Average sneakers sold: 100

We notice in the plot above that there are some drastic jumps in order_amount which is likely causing the AOV to be so high¶

In order to better understand the order_amount it makes sense to look into the factors directly affecting it such as total_items. I also calculate the average sneaker value since that is the other value that could be causing the increased order amounts.¶

Total_items x average sneaker value = order_amount. However, it is worth noting that the relationship might not be this simple as there could be other contributing factors to the order_amount such as shipping, taxes, etc. but that is out of the scope of this analysis and for the sake of this challenge, I will ommit such confounding variables¶

So it is shop_id 78 that sold all its sneakers for the price of 25,725! Surely this can't be the actual price they are selling the sneakers for especially when some people are paying in cash. Since more data is needed to figure out why these numbers are so high, I will ommit shop_id 78 from my calculation. I will not ommit other anomalous orders such as the ones with 2000 items since they do not have unreasonable sneaker prices¶

To calulcate the average across stores, I group the dataframe by shop_id, find the mean of each group and then find the mean of all the means. This method takes into account any adjustments in sneaker price a single shop might make across different orders as well the as the number of order they have made¶

In [11]:
df_trimmed = df[df["shop_id"] != 78]
grouped_df = df_trimmed.groupby("shop_id")
mean_df = grouped_df.mean()
